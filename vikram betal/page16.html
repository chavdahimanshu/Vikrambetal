16. The Clever Barber

  In this story, there was a barber in a small village who was renowned for his wit and cleverness. One day, he overheard a group of ministers discussing the king's plan to build a new palace. The ministers were concerned about the construction cost and suggested that the king consult the clever barber, who was known for his intelligence.

  The king agreed to meet the barber and asked him for advice. The clever barber came up with an ingenious plan. He told the king that if he allowed the barber to build the palace, he could complete the task within a week and at half the cost.

  Intrigued, the king agreed to the barber's proposal. The clever barber started the construction but did something unusual. He built the palace using bricks made of wax. As a result, the palace looked magnificent, but it would melt in the rain.

  When the construction was complete, the king was amazed at the beauty of the palace and praised the barber for his cleverness. However, soon after, the rainy season arrived, and the palace began to melt. The king realized that he had been tricked.

  Betal, who had been listening to this tale, asked King Vikramaditya the question: "What should the king have done differently to avoid being deceived by the clever barber?"

  Vikramaditya thought for a moment and then replied, "The king should have consulted his experienced ministers and architects before making a decision. By relying on the expertise of knowledgeable people, he could have avoided falling for the barber's trick."

  Impressed by Vikramaditya's wisdom, Betal flew back to the tree, and the cycle of stories continued.
