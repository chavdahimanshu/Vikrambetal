4. The Four Princesses

  Once upon a time, in a kingdom ruled by a wise and just king, there were four princesses. These princesses were known throughout the land for their extraordinary beauty and grace. They were not just beautiful on the outside but also kind and virtuous.

  One day, the king decided that it was time for his daughters to get married. He summoned the four princes of neighboring kingdoms and arranged a grand Swayamvara (a ceremony where a princess chooses her husband from among eligible suitors) for his daughters. The Swayamvara was to be held on four consecutive days, with each princess choosing her husband one by one.

The Swayamvara began, and the first princess chose a prince who was known for his intelligence. The second princess chose a prince renowned for his strength and valor. The third princess chose a prince famous for his musical talent, and the fourth princess, the youngest of them all, chose a prince celebrated for his humility and kindness.

  The weddings were celebrated with great pomp and splendor. However, soon after the weddings, a strange incident occurred. Each prince fell seriously ill and seemed to be near death. The king was deeply worried and consulted his ministers and astrologers. They concluded that the sickness was a result of some curse or evil influence.

  Word of the princesses' predicament reached the ears of a wandering sage who was passing through the kingdom. The sage offered to help and asked to see the four princesses. He discovered that each princess had a unique curse on her that was affecting her husband. The sage revealed that the curses were the result of the princesses' past lives, and they could only be broken if certain conditions were met.

The conditions were as follows:



  1. The first princess's husband could only be saved if he found a book of wisdom that nobody else in the kingdom possessed.


  2. The second princess's husband could only be saved if he found a flower that bloomed only once in a century.


  3. The third princess's husband could only be saved if he found a parrot that could sing the most melodious song in the world.


  4. The fourth princess's husband could only be saved if he found a talking monkey with the knowledge of the entire universe.

  The four princes embarked on their respective quests, facing numerous challenges and adventures. With determination and the help of their virtues, they each succeeded in fulfilling the conditions and breaking the curses.

  In the end, the four princesses and their husbands lived happily ever after, and the kingdom rejoiced in their good fortune.

Moral of the Story

  The fourth story of Vikram and Betal highlights the virtues of intelligence, strength, talent, and humility. It emphasizes that true love and dedication can overcome even the most challenging obstacles and curses, and it reinforces the idea that the qualities of a person's character are as important as their external attributes.
