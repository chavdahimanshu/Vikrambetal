22. The Foolish Sage and the Demon

  In this story, there was a sage named Vidyadhar who was renowned for his knowledge and wisdom. One day, he decided to test his abilities by summoning a powerful demon. The demon, upon being summoned, asked the sage, "What can I do for you?"

  The foolish sage, filled with arrogance, said, "I want to test your strength. Can you bring down the sun from the sky?"

  The demon, despite being powerful, was not omnipotent. He replied, "I cannot do that. It's beyond my abilities."

  The sage, thinking the demon was weak, decided to banish him. However, the demon, angered by the sage's arrogance, cast a spell on him. The spell made the sage lose his intelligence and become a fool.

  In his foolish state, the sage wandered to a nearby kingdom. The king of the land, upon learning about the sage's condition, consulted his ministers. One wise minister advised the king to bring the sage to his court and treat him with respect and humility.

  The king followed the minister's advice. He honored the sage, treating him with kindness and regard. Over time, the sage regained his intelligence and wisdom. He realized his earlier arrogance and thanked the king for his compassion.

  Betal, who had been listening to this tale, asked King Vikramaditya a question related to the story. Despite knowing the answer, Vikramaditya remained silent, determined to carry Betal to the sage without uttering a word.

  And thus, the cycle of stories continued, with King Vikramaditya's determination being tested by Betal's tricky questions.
