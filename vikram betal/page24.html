24. The King's Other Wife

  In this story, there was a wise and just king who had two wives. The first wife was kind-hearted and good, while the second wife was envious and wicked. The king loved both of his wives, but he was unaware of the second wife's true nature.

  One day, the first wife discovered the second wife's evil intentions. The second wife planned to kill the king and take over the kingdom with the help of her secret lover, a treacherous minister.

  The first wife, wanting to protect her husband and the kingdom, devised a plan. She sent a message to the king, warning him of the danger he was in. The king, trusting his first wife, took precautions and managed to thwart the plot.

  When the second wife's plan failed, she accused the first wife of treachery. The king, torn between his two wives, didn't know whom to believe. He sought the counsel of his minister, who was wise and perceptive.

  The minister, after careful investigation, discovered the truth. He presented evidence of the second wife's conspiracy and her secret relationship with the treacherous minister. The king, realizing his first wife's loyalty and the second wife's betrayal, banished the wicked queen and her accomplice from the kingdom.

  Betal, who had been listening to this tale, asked King Vikramaditya a question related to the story. Despite knowing the answer, Vikramaditya remained silent, determined to carry Betal to the sage without uttering a word.

  And thus, the cycle of stories continued, with King Vikramaditya's determination being tested by Betal's tricky questions.
