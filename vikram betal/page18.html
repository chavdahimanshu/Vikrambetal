18. The Foolish Brahmin and the Goat

  In this story, a simple-minded Brahmin lived in a village. One day, he bought a goat and decided to take it to a neighboring village to sell. On his way, he met a group of cunning thieves. They hatched a plan to steal the goat from the Brahmin.

  One of the thieves approached the Brahmin and said, "Why are you carrying a dog on your shoulders?"

  The Brahmin, being naive, looked at the goat and thought he had been tricked into buying a useless animal. The thief continued, "This is a magic dog. If you say 'money,' it will produce gold coins."

  Intrigued, the Brahmin followed the thief's instructions and said, "Money!" Of course, the goat didn't produce any gold coins. The Brahmin felt cheated and agreed to sell the goat to the thief for a meager price.

  As the thief led the goat away, another member of the gang asked the Brahmin why he was selling such a wonderful goat. The Brahmin explained what had happened, not realizing that he had been tricked again.

  The second thief decided to try the same trick. He approached the Brahmin and said, "Why are you selling a magic dog? It can produce gold coins if you say 'money.'"

  This time, the Brahmin was smart enough to realize the truth. He replied, "If it's really a magic dog, then why didn't it work when I tried?"

  The second thief was caught off guard and couldn't answer. The Brahmin, now aware of the trick, took his goat back and continued his journey.

  Betal, who had been listening to this tale, asked King Vikramaditya a question related to the story. Despite knowing the answer, Vikramaditya remained silent, determined to carry Betal to the sage without uttering a word.

  And thus, the cycle of stories continued, with King Vikramaditya's determination being tested by Betal's tricky questions.
